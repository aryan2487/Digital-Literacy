Never Scan to Receive: Remember that scanning a QR code is only for sending money; you never need to scan a code or enter your UPI PIN to receive funds.

Verify UPI Identity: Before hitting 'Pay' on any UPI app, double-check the verified name of the recipient to ensure it matches the person or business you intend to pay.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Use 2FA on all primary accounts, especially your college email and social media, to add a second layer of security beyond just a password.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Avoid using the same password across different sites; use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols for each account.

Identify Phishing Links: Do not click on links in SMS or emails that claim your account is "blocked" or offer "free rewards," as these are common tactics to steal login credentials.

Secure Your Hardware: As a student working with microcontrollers and personal laptops, ensure your devices are password-protected and never left unattended in public campus spaces.

Check Website Encryption: Only enter personal or financial information on websites that start with https:// and show a padlock icon in the address bar.

Regularly Update Software: Keep your operating system and apps updated to ensure you have the latest security patches against new vulnerabilities.

One critical step for any person in India is to include the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and the 24x7 Helpline 1930 in their safety resources to ensure immediate action can be taken if a digital or financial scam occurs.
